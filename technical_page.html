Introduction

Performance management is the process of ensuring that a set of activities and outputs meets an organization's goals in an effective and efficient manner.

Application

Performance management principles are used most often in the workplace and can be applied wherever people interact with their environments to produce desired effects.

Performance Management Process

This process is continual, with regular sessions where both management and employees have the opportunity to give and receive feedback.

Performance Planning and Assessing

Agencies develop performance management systems subject to regulations and approval, containing various performance levels.

  • Outstanding
  • Exceeds Fully
  • Fully Successful
  • Minimally Satisfactory
  • Unsatisfactory

How Performance Management Can Fail

In organizational development (OD), performance can be thought of as Actual Results vs Desired Results. Any discrepancy, where Actual is less than Desired, could constitute the performance improvement zone.

Example: PM.failures('unrealistic goals');

Organizational Development

Organizational development involves the practice of planned, systematic change in the values, attitudes, and beliefs of employees within an organization. By focusing on the entire organization, it can apply to changes in strategy, structure, and processes.

Example: OD.initiate('culture change');

In Companies

Performance management in companies refers to the activities, tools, processes, and programs that organizations create or apply to manage the performance of individual employees, teams, departments, and other organizational units.

Example: PM.inCompanies('goal alignment');

Business Performance Management

Business performance management encompasses the processes and strategies that enable an organization to optimize its business performance. It involves the analysis of organizational operations and the implementation of necessary interventions.

Example: BPM.optimize('operations');

Performance Measurement

Performance measurement involves the process of collecting, analyzing, and/or reporting information regarding the performance of an individual, group, organization, system, or component.

Example: PM.measure('annual review');

Why is Performance Management Important?

Performance management is crucial as it helps organizations ensure that their employees' activities and outputs align with the organization's goals. It provides a means of measuring and improving the efficiency and effectiveness of the workforce.

Example: PM.importance('goal alignment');

PDCA

PDCA (plan–do–check–act) is an iterative management method used in business for the control and continuous improvement of processes and products. It is a fundamental method in the practice of continuous improvement.

Example: PDCA.cycle('continuous improvement');
